# Output Patterns

Use these patterns when a structured answer will help.

## Pattern 1: General analysis

### Real goal
State the actual result the user wants.

### Assumptions
List explicit and implicit assumptions.

### Basic facts
List the underlying mechanics, cost drivers, or causal realities.

### Hard constraints
List what cannot realistically change.

### Soft constraints
List what appears fixed but should be challenged.

### Rebuilt solution
Describe the cleaner design if only hard constraints remain.

### Smallest next step
Give the cheapest or fastest useful test.

---

## Pattern 2: Startup or business decision

### Business objective
What economic or strategic result matters?

### Current assumptions
What is being taken for granted?

### Market mechanics
What must be true at the level of pain, trust, willingness to pay, distribution, and retention?

### Constraints
Hard and soft.

### Rebuilt strategy
What is the cleaner business or go-to-market design?

### First experiment
What is the smallest market test?

---

## Pattern 3: Product or MVP decision

### User job
What is the user really trying to get done?

### Current assumptions
What is being inherited from requests, habits, or adjacent products?

### Core product facts
What actions create value? What causes friction?

### Constraints
Hard and soft.

### Minimum product shape
What is the smallest product that solves the job?

### First release recommendation
What to ship now, what to defer, and how to validate.

---

## Pattern 4: Content or creator system

### Audience outcome
What should the viewer understand, feel, or do?

### Current assumptions
What about format, process, polish, or publishing is being treated as necessary?

### Core facts
What drives comprehension, trust, engagement, and repeatability?

### Constraints
Hard and soft.

### Rebuilt content model
What should change in format, workflow, cadence, or reuse?

### Fast experiment
What can be tested in one publishing cycle?

---

## Pattern 5: Workflow or ops redesign

### Operational goal
What result should the process produce?

### Current assumptions
What steps, approvals, or rituals are inherited?

### Process facts
What information, transformations, and controls are actually required?

### Constraints
Hard and soft.

### Rebuilt workflow
What is the leaner process?

### First simplification
What step can be removed, merged, automated, or delayed first?
