# Startup and Business Strategy

Use this file for startup ideas, business models, pricing, positioning, distribution, growth, differentiation, and moat questions.

## Core orientation

Do not start from startup clichés.

Do not default to:

- raise capital
- build community
- do product-led growth
- copy competitor pricing
- add AI
- launch on every channel

unless the decomposition supports it.

Start from:

- painful problem
- urgency
- frequency
- willingness to pay
- switching friction
- trust formation
- distribution mechanics
- unit economics
- retention logic
- defensibility sources

## Reframe common questions

### Is this a good startup idea?

Reframe as:

- Is the problem painful enough?
- Who feels it most acutely?
- How often does it happen?
- What do they do today?
- Why is the current solution insufficient?
- Can value be delivered at attractive economics?

### How do I compete?

Reframe as:

- What is the true basis of competition here?
- Is it cost, speed, trust, workflow fit, integration, brand, switching cost, data, or distribution?
- Which of these are hard realities and which are inherited market structure?

### What is the moat?

Reframe as:
A moat is not a slogan. It may come from:

- proprietary data
- workflow embedding
- switching costs
- cost advantage
- trust or brand
- speed of execution
- unique distribution
- network effects
- ecosystem position
- scarce supply

Do not claim moat from AI by default.

## Pricing decomposition

Break pricing into:

- value created
- value captured
- buyer budget logic
- alternative solutions
- purchase authority
- measurable ROI
- risk reduction
- speed or convenience value

Ask:

- Is pricing copied from the market instead of derived from value mechanics?
- Is the buyer paying for seats, usage, outcome, convenience, speed, money saved, or risk reduced?
- Which pricing variable best matches how value is felt?

## Growth decomposition

Break growth into:

- attention source
- message relevance
- trust formation
- conversion friction
- onboarding friction
- time-to-value
- retention loop
- referral or expansion mechanics

Do not accept "we need more traffic" without identifying the real bottleneck.
